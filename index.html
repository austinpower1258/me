About Me

Austin Wang is a student from Northwood High School in Irvine, California. As an aspiring innovator and researcher, Austin enjoys living in a world of imagination, creating new products to make the world a better place. A proof of concept of one of his projects, Musicizer, won him the Gordon Center Engineering Leadership Award/Fellowship for Best Overall Project and the Startup Award for the Best Innovative Product and Business Plan. Austin is also interested in creative problem-solving in mathematics, physics, and computer science, obtaining many accolades in those respective fields while traveling around the United States to compete in college-run competitions. Austin is also an avid violinist, who has competed in many concerto competitions such as the Redlands Bowl, has received many honors and awards, and is a consistent member of many premier youth orchestras. Currently, Austin is working on independent projects that apply machine learning with online datasets while creating other side projects on his Raspberry Pi. In the future, he wishes to apply his technical knowledge to the field of astrophysics, where he aspires to contribute research to further our understanding of interstellar phenomena and extragalactic supernovae.

Modeling Extragalactic Supernovae

 Monitored and modeled the light curves of recent extragalactic supernovae. Remotely controlled the Leitner Observatory Telescope using TeamViewer for data collection, pending AAVSO approval for publishing for public use.

Musicizer LLC

 A California LLC tech startup based on the idea of a lifestyle-driven, free music-streaming application that uses real-time tempo modification of your music to incentivize running at a consistent pace.
